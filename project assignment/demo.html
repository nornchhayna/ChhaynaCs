Foundation Studies

Typical Components of Foundation Studies
Mathematics:
Topics Covered: Algebra, calculus, statistics, and geometry. Purpose: To develop problem-solving skills and quantitative reasoning, which are crucial for engineering and technology subjects.
Physics:
Topics Covered: Mechanics, electricity and magnetism, thermodynamics, and wave phenomena. Purpose: To understand the physical principles that underpin technological processes and engineering applications.
Chemistry:
Topics Covered: General chemistry, chemical reactions, and material properties. Purpose: To provide knowledge on materials science and chemical processes relevant to engineering disciplines.
Technical Drawing:
Topics Covered: Orthographic projections, isometric drawing, and CAD (Computer-Aided Design). Purpose: To teach students how to create and interpret technical diagrams, which are essential for engineering and design work.
Computer Science:
Topics Covered: Basic programming, algorithms, and data structures. Purpose: To introduce students to computational thinking and programming skills that are increasingly important in various technical fields.
